IPL Ecosystem Map · Reading Guide

How to read
this map

The canvas is not an illustration — it makes an argument made spatial. Every symbol, colour, and motion pattern carries meaning. This page decodes it.

01 — Canvas World

The Visual World

The background canvas is the argument. Zones, vessels, and wave layers represent the structural conditions that produce the gap between education and industry — before any character speaks.

Star Island
Academic Zone
Star shape with amber glow, 6-second floating cycle. The academic world: internally coherent, generative on its own terms. The bob animation shows it is alive — but its motion is self-contained. It does not drift toward the archipelago.
Linh's Ship
Single vessel
One vessel departing Star Island — larger, slower, built for a long journey rather than rapid manoeuvre. The ship represents Linh specifically: a graduating student crossing the Digital Media Ocean toward an industry she has not yet navigated. The ship is alone on the crossing. There is no fleet.
Industry Hub
Archipelago
5–7 islands with a 7.5-second float cycle, offset 3 seconds from Star Island. Industry is not a single destination — it is a dispersed landscape of agencies, studios, and roles. The phase offset means the two worlds are never floating in sync: they move at different rhythms, neither waiting for the other.
10 Boats
Industry vessels
Small, fast boats in and around the Industry Hub. They belong to the industry side — purpose-built for short crossings between islands. They represent the pace culture Tom describes: rapid deployment, multi-directional, iterating between clients and formats without pause. The boats are not waiting to receive Linh's ship. They are already in motion, working a different scale of water.
Ship vs. Boats
Core contrast
The contrast is the argument. Linh's vessel is built for depth and distance; the industry boats are built for speed and range. Neither is the wrong kind of boat — they are vessels designed for different waters. The curriculum's job is not to replace the ship with a boat, but to give Linh the capacity to navigate once she arrives at an archipelago built for smaller, faster craft.
Ocean Waves
6-layer SVG
Two groups of waves scroll in opposing directions at 9 s and 14 s cycles. The gap has depth and competing currents. Parallax layering signals that the Digital Media Ocean is not a flat distance but a layered system in motion — the same water Linh's ship is crossing, and that the industry boats have always known.

02 — Navigation

The Two Layers

The nav bar at the top switches between two readings of the same map. Each layer asks a different question.

Layer 1

The Gap

Shows the situation as it stands. Linh's ship is stuck near Star Island. Three storm nodes occupy the ocean between her and the Industry Hub. Click any node to read the conflict it names.

Layer 2

The Compass

Shows the structural response. Three solution nodes replace the storms. Each has a slider — drag it toward 100% to apply the intervention and watch the connection between Linh and Tom strengthen. Linh's ship advances as solutions are applied.

04 — Interface

Page Chrome

The fixed interface elements outside the canvas also carry meaning. None are decorative.

Concept Note
Canvas overlay
The small note card pinned to the canvas surface. In Layer 1 it reads "Three storms block the crossing" — in Layer 2 it reads "Three solutions clear the path." It frames the narrative register of each layer without appearing in the nav bar.
Compass Rose
Orientation
A nautical compass fixed to the canvas. It marks this as a map to be navigated, not a diagram to be read. It does not point north — it anchors the register of the entire visual world.
Map Legend
Toggle visible
A compact key listing node types. Opens and closes via the legend toggle. Provides quick reference for readers who encounter the map without this Navigator page.

06 — Interaction

How to use the map

01
Explore Layer 1 — The Gap

Click Linh, Tom, or any storm node to open the detail panel. Read who they are, what the conflict is, and what each character's direct quote reveals about their perspective.

02
Switch to Layer 2 — The Compass

Use the nav bar at the top. The storm nodes are replaced by three solution diamonds. The ocean connections are now potential paths, waiting to be activated.

03
Click a solution node and read the intervention

Each diamond opens a panel describing the IPL intervention — what it does, how it works, and what changes for Linh when it is applied.

04
Drag the slider to 100%

The slider inside each solution panel controls the intensity of the intervention. As you move it toward 100%, the arc between Linh and Tom brightens and Linh's ship advances across the ocean.

05
Apply all three solutions

When all three sliders reach 100%, Linh's ship arrives at the Industry Hub. The Resolution panel opens — "The Adaptive Practitioner" — and Linh's reply to Tom becomes visible.

07 — Resolution

The Adaptive Practitioner

The conclusion is present from the start — rendered at low opacity, unreachable until all three interventions are applied. Nothing is added when it unlocks; the act of engaging with the system changes what was already there.

Bridge line
Animated
A line between Linh and Tom draws itself from nothing when the resolution unlocks. The gradient runs amber-to-green: from the student's world through the intervention space. It does not reach all the way to slate — the bridge lands at resolve, not at industry assimilation.
"Bridged" tag
Centre card
A status, not a claim. The artefact does not say the gap is closed. It says the three interventions together constitute a bridge — positioned between both character cards rather than belonging to either world.
Knowledge with Action
Resolution frame
The conclusion reframes the problem. Isolated academic knowledge, when scaffolded by targeted IPL interventions and ethical AI integration, becomes knowledge with action — bridging the classroom and the Asia-Pacific agency ecosystem.
